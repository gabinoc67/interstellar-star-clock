Photon Flux + CST Companion Dashboard

This dashboard is a companion to the paper. It does not claim faster-than-light travel or new physics. It uses standard photon relations and a careful CST extension layer as a control-and-navigation framework: synchronize timing, align with environmental windows, and estimate how photon momentum can support high-speed missions.

Mission Inputs

Reference Equations

Energy: E = h*c/lambda
Momentum: p = E/c
Photon flux: Phi = I/E
Pressure: P_absorb = I/c ; P_reflect = (1 + R)*I/c
Force: F = P*A
Acceleration: a = F/m
Velocity gain: Delta v = a*t
Neutral rhythm: I(t) = I0 + DeltaI_day + DeltaI_season + DeltaI_solar
CST sync variable: DeltaT = T_vehicle - T_reference
Penalty model: J = w1*(DeltaT)^2 + w2*(dDeltaT/dt)^2 + w3*(DeltaI_mismatch)^2

What the CST Extension Means Here

In this dashboard, CST is not treated as a replacement for relativity or electromagnetism. It is used as a control layer. The idea is simple: keep timing mismatch bounded, favor operational windows where photon conditions are stable or favorable, and reduce unnecessary correction burden. That means better synchronization, not faster-than-light travel.

Interpretation Panel

Panel Meaning Why it matters
Photon energy Energy carried by one photon at the chosen wavelength. Sets the conversion from power-per-area into photon count.
Photon flux How many photons arrive per square meter per second. Connects light input to sensing, heating, and momentum budgets.
Radiation pressure Momentum transfer per unit area. Directly determines sail force and long-duration Δv.
CST penalty and sync status A control score derived from timing mismatch and environmental mismatch. Helps interpret whether the craft is operating inside a favorable timing window.
