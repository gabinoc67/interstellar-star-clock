How This CST Time Machine Model Works — Stamp, Lock, Scan, Arrive

What CST adds (the “stamp”): CST is treated as an invariant return timestamp for a chosen Earth-locked worldline event. It’s a control boundary condition: without the stamp, you can hit the right calendar time but miss the correct spacetime event (place+time).

Earth-Locked worldline anchor: Latitude/longitude define the moving Earth reference. The simulator interprets the destination as:

  • Xᵘ_arrival = Xᵘ_Earth(lat,long)(t_dest) (conceptual: arrive on Earth’s worldline at that anchor)
  • Δt = t_dest − t_now (clock difference that must converge to zero)

VGL / VGI safety upgrade:

  • VGL (Variable Gravity Lock) is modeled as a feedback hold that suppresses drift and rotation phase error.
  • VGI scans the target worldline. If an unexpected mass appears above your threshold, or margins collapse, the system safely aborts.

Integrity meter meaning: “Integrity” is a toy score combining stamp phase error + spatial drift + rotation offset. When it stays near 100%, you’re locked to the intended Earth event. If it falls, you are losing lock and should abort.

This remains a conceptual visualization. SR/GR equations shown are real; CST stamp + lock + scan are modeled as a falsifiable control framework (fail-safe behavior, measurable drift/error), while the actuator physics remain to be proven by experiment.
