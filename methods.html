Methods & References

This page documents assumptions, equations, and sources used across calculators (CST timing, warp tunnels, event-horizon planner). Version: v1.0

Assumptions

  • Cosmology: Flat ΛCDM unless noted; default parameters H0 (km·s−1·Mpc−1), Ωm, ΩΛ, Ωk=0.
  • Units: Distances in Gly (1 Gly = 109 ly). Speeds in ly/yr with c = 1 ly/yr.
  • Distance convention: Comoving distance D0 for horizon/expansion calculations.
  • Recession v_rec = H0 · D0 (ly/yr after unit conversion of H0).
  • Effective vehicle speed: v_eff is a design parameter for tunnel/warp closure against the comoving grid; it does not assert local superluminal motion.

Equations

Reachability in Expanding Space

With constant H0 approximation, the comoving separation obeys dD/dt = - v_eff + H0 · D. Reachability requires v_eff > H0 · D0.

Time (given speed)
t = (1/H0) · ln( v_eff / (v_eff − H0·D0) )
Speed (given time T)
v_eff = (H0·D0) / (1 − e^{−H0·T})

Cosmology (from redshift)

Comoving distance from redshift z: D_C = (c/H0) ∫[0→z] dz' / E(z'), where E(z) = sqrt(Ω_m(1+z)^3 + Ω_k(1+z)^2 + Ω_Λ). Our calculators use Simpson integration.

Navigation & Matching

  • Arrival matching: set peculiar velocity to the local flow at destination to remain comoving post-arrival.
  • “Shorter path” tunnels: treat W > 1 path-shortening factor; apparent speed ≈ W · vlocal while keeping vlocal ≤ c.

Differences vs. Prior Work

  • Alcubierre (1994): we prioritize a planning workflow (tools + CST timing) rather than a single bubble metric.
  • Natário (2002): geometry-first insight aligns with our circle-space routing and corridor shaping.
  • Lentz (2021): positive-energy directions are tracked as hypotheses; our calculators remain metric-agnostic.

Reproducibility

  • Parameters: versions, H0, Ω, redshift/D0, mode (speed or time), veff or T.
  • Planned: adapters to external toolchains (e.g., Warp Factory) using exported JSON.
