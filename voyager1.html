Warp-Drive Energy Symmetry Engine

A Theoretical Framework for Anisotropic Stress–Energy, Photon Asymmetry, and CST Timing

Abstract. This demo implements an extended theoretical framework for a warp-drive propulsion concept based on energy–matter symmetry, quantum vacuum dynamics, and temporal synchronization. The model investigates whether controlled photon–pair creation and annihilation cycles, when driven by physically realizable energy systems such as nuclear power sources or plasma propulsion engines, can produce anisotropic stress–energy distributions sufficient to induce directional spacetime curvature. A synchronization parameter, termed Curvature Synchronization Time (CST), is introduced as a phase-control mechanism linking energy cycles to relativistic timing references, including an Earth atomic clock, Coordinated Universal Time (UTC), and interstellar clocks derived from the deep-space probes Voyager 1 and Voyager 2. The framework treats the Earth–Voyager 1–Voyager 2 geometry as the primary timing triangle and uses the atomic clock at Earth as the local precision reference. The proposal remains theoretical and is intended as a testable extension of known physics rather than a validated propulsion model.

Purpose of the Demo

The point of this demo is not to claim that Voyager controls curvature or that interstellar flight has been solved. Its purpose is to show how a long-baseline timing geometry could be used as a conceptual measurement scaffold. Because Voyager 1 and Voyager 2 are both operating in interstellar space on different outward paths, they provide separated reference endpoints for comparing light-time delay, timing drift, and phase mismatch relative to Earth. The atomic clock at Earth is used as the precision origin for those comparisons.

In this model, the triangle helps visualize whether a propulsion system could be evaluated against multiple independent timing baselines instead of a single local clock. The result is a theory-only synchronization map for CST rather than a propulsion proof.

How to Read the Diagram

The lower engine sketch shows a simplified asymmetry chamber. One photon channel is drawn as escaping, while the opposing channel is absorbed, redirected, or delayed. This is only a conceptual way to show how momentum cancellation could be broken inside the model. The top triangle shows Earth at the lower vertex and Voyager 1 and Voyager 2 as the two interstellar endpoints. The atomic clock icon is attached to Earth because Earth is the precision timing origin in the simulation. The moving phase bar and changing readouts represent CST mismatch, not real-time telemetry.
