What the Robot Does

Robot motion: The robot performs a continuous walking cycle. Both legs alternate so one leg moves forward while the other supports, and both arms swing in the opposite direction of the stepping leg like normal human walking.

Head motion: The head turns left and right slightly during the cycle to simulate a natural scanning or balancing behavior.

Torso twist: The torso rotates slightly left and right as the arms and legs alternate, producing a more human-like walking rhythm.

Heel-to-toe stepping: Each foot transitions through heel contact, mid-stance, and toe push-off instead of a simple flat-step motion.

Foot pressure bars: The pressure bars visualize how load shifts through the left and right foot during the walk cycle, especially from heel to forefoot.

Disclaimer

This software is a conceptual simulation and visual demo. It is not a real robotics controller, not a medical gait system, and not a physics-validated locomotion engine.

The memory floors, spider relays, pressure bars, synchronization displays, and walking equations are simplified representations used to illustrate the architecture idea.

This demo should not be used as a real safety, engineering, medical, or industrial control system. It is intended for theory visualization, educational discussion, and prototype-style presentation only.

The motion, memory layers, and relay flows are designed to show how distributed command routing might work in principle, not to claim a finished real-world robotic implementation.
