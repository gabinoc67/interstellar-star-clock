This simulation follows the paper logic that the wheels are inside the ship, while the hull remains non-rotating. Artificial gravity comes from wheel rotation, not from the clocks themselves.

25 meters radius means about 82 feet radius. That gives a full diameter of about 164 feet. At 6 rpm, the wheel makes one complete turn every 10 seconds.

A smaller wheel such as 25 feet radius would need to spin much faster to reach 1 g. That may become uncomfortable for long-duration crew use. Larger wheels usually improve biological comfort.

In this model, atomic time is the external reference clock for ship-level timing, while CST time is the internal control layer used to represent habitat stability, synchronization, and timing offset.
