Pathway 1 — Time as an Active Control Variable

  1. Define a reference time or frame clock.
  2. Measure mismatch using ΔT = T_ship − T_ref.
  3. Impose a stable envelope using |ΔT| ≤ δt.
  4. Extend to many clocks with a synchronization cost function.
  5. Use feedback control to push the system back toward alignment.
  6. Interpret the output as sync quality, drift burden, and lock condition.

Pathway 2 — Geometry as a Diagnostic Surface

  1. Use GR as the geometry-energy baseline.
  2. Represent candidate spacetime structures with a metric form.
  3. Evaluate coordinate-independent curvature invariants.
  4. Identify ridges, low-curvature zones, wakes, and sharp boundary regions.
  5. Translate those geometric signatures into panel outputs or driver targets.

In your framework, geometric plots are not treated as proof of transport capability. They are treated as diagnostic maps that help compare candidate curvature behaviors.

Pathway 3 — Distance, Expansion, and Reachability

  1. Convert redshift or known distance into a comoving distance measure.
  2. Balance effective closing speed against expansion.
  3. Determine whether the target is reachable in the simplified model.
  4. Solve either for travel time or required effective speed.

Pathway 4 — Energy Bookkeeping to Stress-Energy Language

  1. Use E = mc² to express mass-energy equivalence.
