Professional Notes and Disclaimer

Important planning note:

All spending should be entered as honestly and completely as possible, even if spending is currently above take-home pay. A loan is generally not treated as ordinary income for this type of personal budget estimate, but other income that is earned, reported by third parties, or otherwise taxable can affect the annual tax result. If a person reports too little income compared with lifestyle, assets, or spending patterns, that can create serious tax and budgeting problems and may require a licensed tax professional to review the situation.

Audit / recordkeeping caution:

The IRS says audits can be selected by random selection, computer screening, or because a return is connected to other taxpayers or transactions under examination. Audits review records for items such as income, expenses, and deductions. This dashboard is for budgeting and withholding planning only and is not legal or tax representation.

Professional disclaimer:

This software is an educational estimation tool only. It is not the IRS, not tax preparation software, not legal advice, not accounting advice, and not a substitute for a CPA, EA, attorney, payroll department, or licensed tax preparer. Credits, filing status, custody rules, disability treatment, Social Security rules, self-employment taxes, itemized deductions, state taxes, local taxes, premium tax credits, retirement income, capital gains, withholding methods, and special family circumstances can materially change the final tax result. Some smaller credits and special rules may apply in real life and are not fully included here. Always verify your final numbers with a qualified tax professional.
