👴 Social Security Explanation

Simple Social Security Retirement Explanation

If a person is under full retirement age and works while receiving Social Security retirement, benefits can be reduced under the annual earnings test. In 2026, benefits are generally reduced by $1 for every $2 earned above $24,480 if the person is under full retirement age all year, and $1 for every $3 earned above $65,160 in the year the person reaches full retirement age, before the month full retirement age is reached. Reporting expected earnings to Social Security early is usually important because check reductions can affect the following year until full retirement age is reached.
