👴 Social Security Explanation

Simple Social Security Retirement Explanation

If a person is under full retirement age and works while receiving Social Security retirement, benefits can be reduced under the annual earnings test. In 2026, benefits are generally reduced by $1 for every $2 earned above $24,480 if the person is under full retirement age all year, and $1 for every $3 earned above $65,160 in the year the person reaches full retirement age, before the month full retirement age is reached. Starting with the month full retirement age is reached, there is no earnings limit. Reporting expected earnings to Social Security early is usually important because check reductions can affect the following year until full retirement age is reached.

⚠️ Professional Notes and Disclaimer

Important Notes

Tax calculation note:
This planner uses combined taxpayer and spouse wage income, other annual taxable income, combined withholding, 2026 standard deduction, and 2026 marginal rate brackets. It also includes Child Tax Credit and Other Dependent Credit logic. A fully accurate Earned Income Credit or refundable Additional Child Tax Credit calculation requires more data than this form currently collects, so those parts are not fully modeled here.
Social Security note:
The Social Security earnings-test explanation uses a practical age-based estimate because the form does not collect date of birth or birth month. Age 62 through 65 is treated as under full retirement age for the full year, age 66 is treated as the year of reaching full retirement age for estimate purposes, and age 67 or older is treated as full retirement age reached.
