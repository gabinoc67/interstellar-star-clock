Texas All or Nothing Balanced Coverage Generator

This program does not predict All or Nothing. It analyzes draw history, builds balanced number zones, avoids cloned tickets, adds controlled randomness, and generates 40 coverage tickets with explanations.

All or Nothing Draw History Input

Paste at least 50 to 100 past All or Nothing draws. One draw per line. Each line should contain 12 unique numbers from 1 through 24. There is no bonus ball for this game.

Important: This is a coverage and distribution tool, not a guaranteed prediction tool. All or Nothing is random. The purpose is to reduce repeated combinations, avoid narrow number traps, and cover more possible draw shapes. Because every ticket uses 12 out of 24 numbers, anti-clone filtering is stricter and must focus on reducing repeated full-ticket structure.

Algorithm Settings

Zones: 1–4, 5–8, 9–12, 13–16, 17–20, 21–24.

Program Equations Used Before Ticket Output

Frequency Score: how often each number appeared in the uploaded history.

Recency Score: newer draws receive a small weight so the program can detect recent movement without overfitting.

Zone Balance: each 12-number ticket should spread across all six zones instead of collapsing into one side of the board.

Spread Score: the ticket is rewarded when it covers both low and high numbers.

Anti-Clone Rule: a ticket is rejected if it shares too many numbers with already selected tickets.

Entropy Rule: some numbers must come from outside the hottest numbers to avoid historical echo traps.
