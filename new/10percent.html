Disclaimer

This is a theoretical educational demo, not a medical test, IQ test, diagnosis, or neuroscience measurement device. It does not measure real neurons or real synapses. It models the idea that intelligence improvement comes from better connection quality, recall practice, feedback, sleep, challenge, and repeated training.

The model assumes the human brain has about 86 billion neurons, while intelligence is represented here as network efficiency, not “using more of the brain.”

Core Theory

Clear rule: You do not upgrade intelligence by increasing total connections. You upgrade performance by improving connection quality and efficiency.

Model: Intelligence Growth ≈ Practice × Recall × Feedback × Sleep × Challenge

Birth → Loose web | Childhood → Overbuilt web | Adult → Optimized web | Aging → Reorganization or decline

Training Guidance

  • Recall: Test yourself without looking at the answer.
  • Practice: Repeat the skill over several days.
  • Feedback: Correct mistakes immediately.
  • Sleep: Rest so memory can consolidate.
  • Challenge: Use questions slightly harder than your comfort level.
  • Consistency: Improvement comes from repeated activation of useful pathways.
