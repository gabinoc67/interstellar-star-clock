CST Neural Connection Training Simulator

Disclaimer

This is a theoretical educational demo, not a medical test, IQ test, diagnosis, or neuroscience measurement device. It does not measure real neurons or real synapses. It models the idea that intelligence growth may improve through connection quality, recall practice, feedback, sleep, challenge, and repeated training.

Short Theory Explanation

Based on the Academia paper logic, intelligence growth is modeled as better brain-network efficiency, not simply “using more of the brain.” The simulator treats practice, recall, feedback, sleep, and challenge as training forces that may improve connection quality.

The user takes 20 questions, reviews the correct answers, studies weak areas, and repeats the test to compare improvement.

Core Rule

You do not upgrade intelligence by increasing total connections.

You upgrade performance by improving connection quality, organization, timing, recall, and efficiency.

Training Guidance

  • Use recall instead of only rereading.
  • Correct mistakes immediately with feedback.
  • Sleep after learning to support memory consolidation.
  • Use moderate challenge, not impossible challenge.
  • Repeat the test to compare first and second attempt.

20 Question Neural Training Test
