Interpretation

The simulation follows the idea that human survival creates networks. Bones show where early humans survived. Cities show where people stored food, memory, calendars, trade, and knowledge. Collapse events show where the system overloaded from drought, war, population pressure, weak supply lines, water failure, volcanic events, disease, or economic instability. Each reset can destroy one node but also move knowledge to the next generation.

The geometry is not proof of a hidden global grid. It is a systems model showing how routes, coastlines, rivers, fertile land, climate belts, and trade paths naturally create triangles, crossing points, hubs, and migration corridors.
