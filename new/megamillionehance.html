Mega Millions Balanced Coverage Generator

This program does not predict Mega Millions. It analyzes draw history, separates white-ball and Mega Ball bonus-ball behavior, builds balanced zones, avoids cloned tickets, adds controlled randomness, and generates 40 coverage tickets with explanations.

Mega Millions Draw History Input

Paste at least 50 to 100 past Mega Millions draws. One draw per line. Each line should contain 5 white balls from 1 through 70 plus 1 Mega Ball from 1 through 24. Put the Mega Ball bonus number last.

Important: This is a coverage and distribution tool, not a guaranteed prediction tool. Mega Millions is random. The purpose is to reduce repeated combinations, avoid narrow number traps, and cover more possible draw shapes.

Algorithm Settings

White-Ball Zone Balance: 1–70

Zones: 1–7, 8–14, 15–21, 22–28, 29–35, 36–42, 43–49, 50–56, 57–63, 64–70.

Mega Ball Bonus Zone Balance: 1–24

Bonus zones: 1–4, 5–8, 9–12, 13–16, 17–20, 21–24.

Program Equations Used Before Ticket Output

White-Ball Frequency Score: how often each white ball appeared in the uploaded history.

Mega Ball Frequency Score: how often each bonus ball appeared separately from the white balls.

Recency Score: newer draws receive a small weight so the program can detect recent movement without overfitting.

Spread Score: the ticket is rewarded when white balls avoid being too compressed or too stretched.

Anti-Clone Rule: a ticket is rejected if it shares too many white balls with already selected tickets.

Entropy Rule: some white balls and bonus balls must come from outside the hottest numbers.
