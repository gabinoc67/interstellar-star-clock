The first stage reads Brownsville launch weather, outside temperature, wind speed, fuel condition, oxidizer flow, heat, and vibration. Damping control keeps the burn stable so energy becomes useful thrust.

Stage 1: Booster / Air Takeoff

Starts at the launch pad. Thick air, gravity, and wind require stronger thrust and damping.

Stage 2: Hybrid Main Engine

Engages during climb. Fuel and oxidizer flow are adjusted as air density drops.

Stage 3: Upper Atmosphere Stage

Engages near thin air. Stored oxidizer, chamber pressure, heat shielding, and damping increase.

Stage 4: Vacuum Space Stage

Engages in space. No outside air is available, so thrust depends on carried propellant and nozzle efficiency.

Educational Safety Note

This is a visual educational simulation, not a flight-certified rocket design. Real propulsion requires combustion testing, structural testing, thermal analysis, guidance control, range safety, and professional review.
