The engine begins by reading air density, fuel condition, oxidizer flow, heat buildup, and vibration. Damping control keeps the burn smoother so more energy becomes useful thrust instead of wasted vibration and unstable pressure waves.

Air Takeoff

Uses thick air, strong thrust, fuel reading, and damping to control early vibration.

Climb

Air gets thinner, heat rises, oxidizer control becomes more important, and damping prevents pulsing.

Upper Atmosphere

The system shifts toward stored oxidizer, nozzle control, and thermal protection.

Outer Space

No air is available, so the rocket depends fully on stored oxidizer, fuel, nozzle expansion, and cold protection.

This is an educational concept simulation. It explains a possible hybrid rocket process in simple engineering language. Real rocket design requires tested propulsion hardware, safety certification, thermal analysis, combustion modeling, materials testing, and flight validation.
