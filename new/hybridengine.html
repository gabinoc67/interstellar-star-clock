Why 1% Light Speed Matters

At 1% light speed, Mars becomes reachable in about 20.8 hours using the average-distance estimate.

This could improve crew safety by reducing radiation exposure, increase mission flexibility, support emergency resupply, and make Mars feel reachable instead of distant.

Research Meaning

This model shows a realistic intermediate step: a synchronized hybrid propulsion architecture designed to improve energy use, reduce trip time, and keep propulsion subsystems coordinated by clock timing. The goal is not instant warp, but controlled high-energy propulsion.
Nuclear = baseline power
Ion = efficient long cruise
Plasma = short correction burst
CST = synchronization layer
Sensors = proof layer
Scientific Disclaimer: This simulator does not claim near-light-speed travel is currently achievable, does not claim real warp capability, and does not claim validated spacetime curvature control. It is a conceptual engineering visualization showing how a hybrid power and propulsion stack could be organized, synchronized, and measured for future high-efficiency interplanetary mission research.
