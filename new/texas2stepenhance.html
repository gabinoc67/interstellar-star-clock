Texas Two Step Balanced Coverage Generator

This program does not predict Texas Two Step. It analyzes draw history, separates the 4 main numbers and Bonus Ball behavior, builds balanced zones, avoids cloned tickets, adds controlled randomness, and generates 40 coverage tickets with explanations.

Texas Two Step Draw History Input

Paste at least 50 to 100 past Texas Two Step draws. One draw per line. Each line should contain 4 main numbers from 1 through 35 plus 1 Bonus Ball from 1 through 35. Put the Bonus Ball last.

Important: This is a coverage and distribution tool, not a guaranteed prediction tool. Texas Two Step is random. The purpose is to reduce repeated combinations, avoid narrow number traps, and cover more possible draw shapes.

Algorithm Settings

Main Number Zone Balance: 1–35

Zones: 1–7, 8–14, 15–21, 22–28, 29–35.

Bonus Ball Zone Balance: 1–35

Bonus zones: 1–7, 8–14, 15–21, 22–28, 29–35.

Program Equations Used Before Ticket Output

Main Frequency Score: how often each main number appeared in the uploaded history.

Bonus Frequency Score: how often each Bonus Ball appeared separately from the main numbers.

Recency Score: newer draws receive a small weight so the program can detect recent movement without overfitting.

Spread Score: the ticket is rewarded when main numbers avoid being too compressed or too stretched.

Anti-Clone Rule: a ticket is rejected if it shares too many main numbers with already selected tickets.

Entropy Rule: some main numbers and bonus balls must come from outside the hottest numbers.
