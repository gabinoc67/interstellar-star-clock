HEURISTIC VS PROOF

A heuristic is a method, pattern, or idea that appears to work and helps find answers, but does not prove the answer is always true.

Heuristic: finds patterns, predicts results, matches known data, suggests where the next zero might be, and gives evidence.

Proof: must show for every nontrivial zero ρ of ζ(s), Re(ρ) = 1/2, and no zero can exist at σ = 0.49, 0.51, 0.60, 0.40, etc.

Stronger test: generate zero #1, #2, #3, continue forever, compare against published zeros, show error approaches zero, and show why off-line zeros are impossible.
