Einstein–CST Linearized Convergence Sandbox

Live toy simulation of a linearized GR-style residual model with a CST-inspired feedback layer. This dashboard is for convergence behavior, control response, and panel interaction — not a full nonlinear general relativity solver.

Simulation Inputs & Tolerances

Idle — awaiting run
What “Steps (N)” does
Total number of simulation updates. More steps give the residual and energy panels more time to settle. Higher values make the run longer and smoother.
What “Δt” does
The time increment per step. Larger Δt makes decay happen faster per frame. Smaller Δt makes the live curves move more gently.
What the tolerances do
εGR, εQM, and target ΔC final decide when the run is considered converged. If the final residuals stay above these, the validation row will fail.
What initial residuals do
RGR(0), RQM(0), and ΔC(0) set how far the system starts from its target. Bigger starting values make the residual plots and energy demand larger.
What spectrum imbalance does
This controls how “uneven” the synthetic h-spectrum starts. It changes the pre/post spectrum panels and the spectral-improvement test.
What kp and kd do
These are the CST-style feedback gains. Higher kp speeds convergence. Higher kd damps changes and stabilizes the run. They strongly affect the residual, energy, and validation panels.
Equations

Linearized GR + CST-Inspired Control Layer

Linearized Einstein equation in trace-reversed form
\[ \Box \bar{h}_{\mu\nu} = -\frac{16\pi G}{c^4} \left( T_{\mu\nu}^{\mathrm{class}} + \langle \hat{T}_{\mu\nu}\rangle + T_{\mu\nu}^{(\mathrm{vac})} + F_{\mu\nu}^{\mathrm{CST}} \right). \] \[ \bar{h}_{\mu\nu} = h_{\mu\nu} -\frac{1}{2}\eta_{\mu\nu} h, \qquad h = \eta^{\alpha\beta} h_{\alpha\beta}. \] Vacuum reference subtraction
\[ T_{\mu\nu}^{(\mathrm{vac})} = \langle 0|\hat{T}_{\mu\nu}|0\rangle - \langle 0|\hat{T}_{\mu\nu}|0\rangle_{\mathrm{flat}}. \] Toy quantum sector
\[ \dot{\hat{\rho}} = -\frac{i}{\hbar}[H_{\mathrm{eff}},\hat{\rho}] + \kappa \mathcal{D}[a]\hat{\rho} + \gamma_{\phi}\mathcal{D}[a^\dagger a]\hat{\rho}. \]
CST feedback law
\[ F_{\mu\nu}^{\mathrm{CST}} = -k_p\,\Delta h_{\mu\nu} -k_d\,\Delta \dot{h}_{\mu\nu}, \qquad \Delta h_{\mu\nu} = h_{\mu\nu}-h_{\mu\nu}^{\mathrm{target}}(t_{\mathrm{CST}}). \] Special relativity timing factor
\[ \gamma_{\mathrm{SR}}(v) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-\dfrac{v^2}{c^2}}}, \qquad t'_{\mathrm{SR}} = \frac{t_{\mathrm{CST}}}{\gamma_{\mathrm{SR}}}. \] Schwarzschild-style clock factor
\[ \Gamma_{\mathrm{GR}}(r) = \sqrt{1-\frac{2GM}{rc^2}}, \qquad t'_{\mathrm{GR}} = t_{\mathrm{CST}}\Gamma_{\mathrm{GR}}. \] Sidereal drift and direction unit vector
\[ \Delta t_{\mathrm{sid}} = N_{\mathrm{days}}(T_{\odot}-T_{\star}), \qquad \mathbf{n}(\alpha,\delta) = (\cos\delta\cos\alpha,\ \cos\delta\sin\alpha,\ \sin\delta). \]
Relativistic energy reference
\[ E_{\mathrm{tot}}=\gamma_{\mathrm{SR}}mc^2, \qquad K=(\gamma_{\mathrm{SR}}-1)mc^2. \] In this sandbox, the energy panel does not represent a real engine. It shows a computed control input power, curvature-response proxy, and efficiency proxy so you can see whether the live run is damping and converging.
