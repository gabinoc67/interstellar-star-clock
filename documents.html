Gabino Casanova Research Portal

This page organizes the document collection into a structured research library. Some documents are clearly theory-driven, meaning they explore conceptual models, speculative frameworks, or proposed systems that are not yet experimentally validated. Other documents are more grounded in known physics, mathematics, or engineering-style interpretation, where the ideas are tied more closely to equations, established physical laws, or technical reasoning. This distinction is shown directly in the document type labels so readers can quickly understand whether a document is primarily exploratory, equation-centered, historical, AI-focused, philosophical, or more physics-grounded.

Theory and Exploratory Documents

These documents are best read as conceptual proposals, interpretive models, or early-stage frameworks. They can be useful for idea generation, long-form thought development, or building new hypotheses, but they should not automatically be treated as validated results.

Physics-Grounded and Technical Documents

These documents are closer to known scientific structure. They may rely on mathematics, equation stacks, engineering analogies, physical interpretation, or more formal reasoning. They still may contain speculative extensions, but they are presented with a stronger tie to conventional physics language.
