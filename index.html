True E(t) vs. Taylor n=1, n=2, n=3

  • True E(t): The full energy predicted by the CST warp-field equation for your chosen parameters (κ, Ω, V, φ(t)).
  • Taylor n=1: First-order (linear) approximation around an operating time t₀.
  • Taylor n=2: Adds curvature/acceleration information via E''(t₀) — a stability indicator.

Why a Page Can “Freeze” (and Easy Fixes)
