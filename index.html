How the Arrow Shield Uses Photons

Photons carry momentum p=E/c. Natural starlight is too weak to push debris, so the Arrow Shield uses onboard lasers/microwave in three ways:

  1. Ionization cone: Light ionizes dust, then magnetic fields deflect charged grains.
  2. Ablation nudges: Pulses create plumes on larger bits, giving them a small lateral Δv.
  3. Range-gated control: Sensors widen the cone, pulse, or adjust heading as needed.
