Sub-C Synchronization (CST-Lock)

The synchronization model treats timing as a control variable. In this framework, Earth-based cadence, onboard timing, destination timing, and high-precision synchronization references are compared continuously to minimize drift and keep the simulated vehicle state aligned to a chosen temporal baseline.

  • Universal Magnetic Frequency drive: oscillatory field cadence near Earth Schumann-style reference timing (~7.83 Hz conceptually used as a cadence anchor).
  • Casanova Synchronized Time (CST): keeps shipboard timing phase-locked to Earth rhythms in the control model.
  • Residual timing error: mapped to coil current commands, rim steepness, effective radius, and pulse phase.
  • Simulator role: used to demonstrate how time-locking could affect throttle, safety, and curvature shaping in theory.

+E / −E Pairing & QEI Compliance

The intent of this section is theoretical: to describe a controller that attempts to satisfy Hawking–Ellis-inspired constraints statistically while using localized negative-energy pulses only within QEI-aware bounds.

  • Paired energy channels: route −E micro-pulses with time-symmetric +E through a high-Q superconducting loop.
  • Target recycling efficiency: η ≥ 0.9999 in the theory model.
  • Balanced curvature nozzle: matched +/− curvature rims intended to keep the interior approximately Minkowski-flat.
  • Control action: if EC or QEI proxies exceed thresholds, lower warp factor and widen rim width σ to reduce |∇Φ|.

Safety Proxies

  • Horizon risk: if gtt ≤ 0, auto-throttle or inhibit the state.
  • Tidal and jerk limits: widen the rim and reduce ramp aggressiveness.
  • Energy budget cap: reactor or power-system ceiling limits curvature authority.
  • Energy-condition proxies: WEC, SEC, and NEC act as practical simulator gating signals.
  • Path-speed cap: ETA depends on path length and any reductions demanded by EC/QEI proxy constraints.

Trajectory & Motion (Simulation Layer)

In simulator versions of the framework, motion is represented with a quadratic Bezier path from Earth to target, a warp-sensitive flattening rule, jerk-limited progress, and arrival-state transitions. This is a visualization and control-logic demonstration, not evidence that a real warp corridor exists or can presently be engineered.
