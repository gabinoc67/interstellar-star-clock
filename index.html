Time Dilation & CST — The Short Version

Time dilation means a moving clock can tick slightly slower than a clock at rest with you. At the modest local speeds I use for context—192 km/s ≈ 0.064% of light speed—the effect is negligible: even over six months, the difference is only a few seconds. Inside a CST-synchronized warp tunnel, the ship stays nearly at rest in its local spacetime and we groom fields to keep gravity gentle, so both special- and gravitational-dilation stay tiny. We arrive sooner by shortening the route, not by breaking the speed of light.

  • Plain idea: We don’t go locally FTL; we reduce the coordinate distance with a curved tunnel guided by CST.
  • Numbers: v/c ≈ 0.000640 → γ ≈ 1.000000205 → seconds of difference over months.
  • Clocks in step: CST = atomic clocks + two-way laser links + pulsar timing to keep Earth and ship clocks aligned.
