What These Satellite Names Mean

GOES = Geostationary Operational Environmental Satellite
Continuous weather monitoring and severe-storm tracking.
GPS = Global Positioning System
Positioning, navigation, and timing.
AEHF = Advanced Extremely High Frequency
Protected, secure, jam-resistant military communications.
MUOS = Mobile User Objective System
Mobile military UHF voice and data communications.
SBIRS = Space Based Infrared System
Missile warning, missile defense support, battlespace awareness.
WGS = Wideband Global SATCOM
Wideband military communications.
ISS = International Space Station
Microgravity research laboratory in low Earth orbit.
OneWeb / Starlink / Iridium / SES / Intelsat / ViaSat / Eutelsat
Commercial communications, broadband, mobile voice/data, and network connectivity.
Landsat / Sentinel / NOAA
Earth observation, land imaging, climate, and weather data.
Position shown here
The map point is the sub-satellite point directly beneath the spacecraft on Earth.
Why speeds look different:
LEO satellites usually move near ~7 to 8 km/s, MEO is slower, and GEO orbital speed is around ~3 km/s while appearing almost fixed over longitude on the map.
Why some satellites barely move on the map:
Geostationary satellites still move in space, but their ground position stays near one longitude because they match Earth’s rotation.
Public display rule used here:
Only publicly cataloged satellites are included. Nothing classified or unlisted is added.
